One of the oldest games humans still play — set out on a board in Mesopotamia around 2600 BCE and dug up at the ancient city of Ur — now running natively on a whole shelf of retro 8-bit machines, from one shared game core.

Features

  • 2-player hot-seat on one machine, or 1 player vs the computer with selectable difficulty (Easy / Normal / Hard).
  • The real Standard-of-Ur look on every port — gold flower rosettes, bullseye eyes, quincunx studs, round two-tone pieces.
  • Chip music & sound, including the Hurrian Hymn title theme — the oldest substantially-complete notated melody known (Ugarit, c.1400 BCE). The Apple II build even drives a Mockingboard if you have one.
  • Cross-platform online play over FujiNet on the Atari and Coleco Adam builds — an Atari can play a real opponent through a live, server-authoritative game server (a Game Boy-vs-C64 future is the whole point of the shared core).
  • Free & open source — every platform, and the server, on GitHub.

How to play

Race your 7 pieces along a winding 14-square path and off the board. On your turn you roll four binary dice (0–4), then move one piece that many squares.

  • 🌺 Rosettes (the flower squares): land on one for an extra roll, and your piece there is safe.
  • ⚔️ The shared middle lane is the combat zone — land exactly on an opponent's piece there and it's sent back to the start.
  • 🎯 You must roll the exact number to bear a piece off the end.
  • 🏆 First to bear off all 7 pieces wins.

Controls

  • Atari 8-bit, C64, Apple II (keyboard): pick menu items and moves by number; press any key to roll.
  • Atari 5200: the controller keypad numbers.
  • Coleco Adam / ColecoVision: keypad to choose, FIRE to roll/confirm.
  • Master System, Game Gear, Game Boy, NES: D-pad up/down moves the cursor, the action button rolls & confirms.

Full per-machine details are in HOW-TO-PLAY.txt inside the download.

About & credits
