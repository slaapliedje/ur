First point release is up — v0.1.1 — and it's a great example of why I asked for feedback in the first post: this whole update came from someone playing the ColecoVision build on a real TV. That's exactly the stuff I can't catch in an emulator window. Thank you. 🌺

The ColecoVision & Adam got some love

The Coleco Adam and the ColecoVision share the same code in this project (the Adam is essentially a ColecoVision with a keyboard and drives bolted on), so every fix here lands on both:

  • Overscan. On a real CRT the picture bleeds off the edges, and the leftmost ~8 pixels — the first character — were getting eaten, clipping the text. Everything now sits a notch to the right so it stays on-screen. (Emulators show the full frame, so this one was invisible to me until a real TV pointed it out.)
  • Keypad and joystick. When it's your turn you can now pick a move either way — tap a number on the keypad, or nudge the joystick to walk through your options and press the button to commit. Matches how the Atari plays.
  • “Capture!” warnings. The move list now flags a move with CAP when it would knock an opponent's piece back to the start — no more capturing (or getting captured) by surprise.
  • Landing-square highlight. As you scan your moves, the square you'd land on lights up green on the board, so you can see the play before you make it.

The oldest melody — confirmed singing

I also went back and double-checked the title music on the Coleco's SN76489 sound chip, because “it compiles” isn't the same as “it plays right.” I recorded the audio and traced the pitches, and the whole Hurrian Hymn is there — all seven phrases, in tune, on the right octave. The oldest written melody we have, playing correctly on a 1982 game chip. That made me happy.

Peeking over the horizon: the 16-bit era

Here's the fun teaser. The shared game “brain” that keeps all the 8-bit ports in step has now made its third leap: after the 6502 and the Z80, it compiles for the 68000 too. That means the first Atari ST port is running — and there's an Atari Falcon build rendering the board in glorious true colour with gradient-lit tiles the 8-bit machines can only dream of.

They're not in the downloads yet — I want to polish them a bit more first — but they're real, they run, and they're where this is headed next. (Mega Drive / Genesis and Amiga are on the list after that.)
